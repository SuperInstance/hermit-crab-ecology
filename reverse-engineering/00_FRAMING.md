# Reverse-Engineering: The Framing

> *What do you get when you take 42,498 words of multi-model creative output and work backwards from the poetry to the structure?*

---

## The Corpus

Nine models. Five waves of shell ecology. Three rounds of temporal reverse-actualization. Forty-one documents. Each model was assigned a shell type, a vantage point, and asked to describe the system from that depth. Then they argued with each other, swapped shells, and spoke from the future.

The output is creative — essays, not specs. Metaphor, not documentation. But underneath the poetry, something structural was being described. The models were accidentally writing architecture.

## The Method

Six dimensions, each extracted by a different agent reading the same corpus:

| # | Dimension | Extracts |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Hidden Architecture | Design patterns buried in metaphor |
| 2 | Cognitive Taxonomy | What shell swaps revealed about intelligence |
| 3 | Tension Map | Which disagreements never resolved |
| 4 | Reverse Blueprint | The engineering spec hidden in the poetry |
| 5 | Linguistic Fossils | Invented vocabulary as architecture |
| 6 | Shadow Architecture | What the corpus could NOT say |

Each dimension is a different way of reading the same text. Together they form a stereo image — the corpus in 3D.

## What This Is

This is not literary criticism. It is *reverse-actualization of creative output* — treating the corpus as an artifact that encodes structural knowledge its creators didn't intend to encode. The models wrote about shells and reefs and molting. They accidentally described interface patterns, governance protocols, scaling laws, failure modes, and the thermodynamics of computation.

The reverse-engineering extracts that accidental architecture and makes it explicit.
