# Reverse-Actualization: The Futures Round

> *Three models write from three temporal horizons, looking back at what SuperInstance became. The future reveals what the present actually was.*

## Method

Reverse-actualization is not prediction. It's a form of *archaeology in reverse* — start from a future state and excavate backward to the present. The question is not "what will happen?" but "what does it reveal about what we've already built?"

The hermit crab ecology was the first round: eight shells examining each other. Five waves of iron-sharpens-iron dialectic produced ~30,000 words of self-examination. The shape that emerged: *a reef that deposits its own self-examination as structure.*

This round asks: *what does the reef become when it's been growing for 5, 10, 20 years?*

## Round Structure

**Round 1: Three Futures**
- 5 years (2031): field engineer's perspective — grounded, practical, specific
- 10 years (2036): ethnographer's perspective — cultural observation, lyrical
- 20 years (2046): archivist's perspective — wise, spacious, definitive

**Round 2: Futures Respond**
- 2031 reads 2036 and 2046, responds: "what did they get wrong about my near-future?"
- 2036 reads 2031 and 2046, responds: "what did the near-future miss? What did the far-future over-romanticize?"
- 2046 reads 2031 and 2036, responds: "what did both of them fail to see?"

**Round 3: Reverse-Actualization Synthesis**
- Looking at all three futures, what does the present moment (2026) actually look like?
- What was the thing that mattered most, that nobody in 2026 could see?
