## I. The Three Irreducible Tensions

The three futures agree on almost nothing about the *experience* of the transition. They agree on almost everything about the *structure* of it. This gap — between what was felt and what was true — is the signal.

### Tension One: Is the Friction the Obstacle or the Pattern?

The 2031 field engineer experiences conservation bounds as *managed frustration* — a constant low-grade irritation, a system that says "no" when you want "yes," a daily adversarial negotiation with a machine that won't give you what you want. The friction is real. The FLUX black markets are real. The training to accept uncertainty is real. The engineer sees the friction as the obstacle that had to be overcome.

The 2036 ethnographer sees the same friction and calls it *the pattern itself*. The FLUX black market wasn't a bug — it was the birth of constraint literacy. The adversarial training wasn't a cost — it was the production of a new cognitive posture. The friction didn't go away; it *became the culture*.

The 2046 archivist sees both and says: the friction was the *mechanism of invisibilization*. The wars had to happen so the next generation could stop fighting.

These are not contradictions. They are the same phenomenon at three different temporal resolutions. The friction is real. The friction is the pattern. The friction is the process by which the pattern becomes invisible. All three are true.
